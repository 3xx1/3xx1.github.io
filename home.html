Welcome to Kaz's Portfolio

Hidekazu Saegusa (Kaz) is an interface creator exploring ways to design more seamless technologies in everyday life. His mission is to move users from a technology-centered world toward a more human-centered world by designing interfaces that augment rather than 'fix'/determine human activity. He usually employs objects in everyday life as interfaces, using his hardware/software building skills, so that users can harness their natural intuitions and utilize a technology with less stress. Eventually, he is trying to design a technology whose existence can be completely ignored by users but still helps improve people's quality of life.

Kaz is currently located in Seattle WA, and working on his second master’s degree in the department of Human Centered Design and Engineering at University of Washington. His design philosophy is to always squeeze at least one “off the wall” idea while brainstorming to solve a problem. He believes this way of thinking usually offers a deeper dive into the design problem and consequently produces more compelling design outcome. His works have been presented and exhibited in major international/domestic academic conferences, such as Advances in Computer Entertainment, Human Robotic Interaction, SIG Computer-Human Interaction, and etc.
